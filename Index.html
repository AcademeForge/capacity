Capacity
The quiet signal that tells the
people you love how you really are.
Capacity is a private, feed-free app that lets your closest circle — partner, family, best friends — see your real energy level at a glance, so nobody has to ask "how are you?" and nobody has to perform an answer. One tap, one color, no feed.

The Problem

"How are you?" is broken. We ask it out of habit and never answer it honestly. When a partner comes home drained and their spouse unloads a to-do list, it's not cruelty — it's missing information. There's no lightweight, honest, real-time signal for emotional bandwidth between people who love each other, so misreads happen constantly and silently accumulate into resentment.

Why Users Download It

Almost everyone has had this specific experience this week: wished the person they live with or love just knew before asking anything of them. The mechanic — tap a state, see their state — needs zero tutorial. It solves a named, felt, recurring pain and promises relief without a single conversation. That's a same-day download.

Why They Return Daily

The use cases — checking your partner's state before calling, updating yours after a hard meeting — recur multiple times a day by nature. It's not a habit you build; it's a reflex that replaces the "how are you" text. The glance becomes as automatic as checking the weather before you leave the house.

The Viral Loop

The app is useless alone. Every new user must invite at least one person to unlock the home screen — that's the invite gate in onboarding step 2. Happy couples expand to families. Families add friends. Because you can only find people by username (no phone number exposed), the share link and username search are the two natural spread vectors, both low-friction and privacy-respecting.

The USP

No feed No content No likes No streaks No performance Username-only privacy One tap to update Quiet notifications

It is structurally the opposite of Instagram — a private utility, not a public performance. That positioning is both the product's identity and its defensible moat against Big Tech, whose ad businesses require engagement and attention Capacity explicitly refuses to manufacture.

Why Users Pay

The free tier is complete for one relationship. Premium ($3.99/mo or $47.99/yr) unlocks:

  • Unlimited circles — family AND friends AND work
  • Custom states — rename and recolor to match your relationship's vocabulary
  • 30-day trends — spot patterns ("Jamie is stretched every Sunday")
  • Calendar sync — auto-sets your state from your schedule, zero effort

Users pay because the free tier proves the value so clearly that unlocking it for every relationship they care about is an obvious next step.

Why It Can Be a Billion-Dollar Company

Every close relationship on earth is the market. At even 5M paying households at $4/mo that's $240M ARR — and the mechanics generalize globally across every culture that has close relationships. The caregiving vertical (elderly parents + adult children) commands 3–5× higher willingness to pay and is massively underserved. Aggregated, anonymized state-pattern data is a research and wellness industry asset. None of this requires advertising.

Why It's Hard to Copy

Big Tech can't build this faithfully — their entire revenue model requires feeds, engagement, and attention that Capacity structurally refuses to manufacture. A clone by Meta would have ads, read receipts, and engagement mechanics within months — which breaks the product's core promise. The real moat is the small trusted circle graph: once your partner, parents, and best friends are in, switching means re-recruiting every person you love. Nobody does that.
